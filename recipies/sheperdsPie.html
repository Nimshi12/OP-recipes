Shepherd's Pie

Gather the family round with this quick and easy shepherd's pie recipe. The meat mixture can be made ahead and frozen. You can also substitute instant potatoes for the real thing if you're in a hurry.

Zippy Shepherd's Pie is a flavorful twist on the classic comfort dish, combining layers of seasoned ground meat, such as beef or turkey, with a zesty tomato or barbecue sauce. It's topped with a creamy layer of mashed potatoes and baked until golden and bubbly.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 (14 ounce) can beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon mild curry powder
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tomatoes, coarsely chopped
  • 1 (16 ounce) package frozen mixed peas and carrots
  • ¼ cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 cup shredded extra-sharp Cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the chopped potatoes in salted water until tender. Drain and mash them with milk, butter, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat canola oil over medium heat. Add ground beef, onions, red bell pepper, and garlic. Cook until the beef is browned and vegetables are soft.
  4. Add beef broth, ketchup, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, curry powder, and cornstarch to the skillet. Stir until well combined and simmer for 5–10 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  5. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and frozen peas and carrots. Cook for another 2–3 minutes until the vegetables are heated through.
  6. Transfer the beef mixture into a baking dish. Spread the mashed potatoes evenly on top.
  7. Top with shredded extra-sharp Cheddar cheese and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Let the Shepherd's Pie cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!
