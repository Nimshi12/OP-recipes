Baked Ziti

This baked ziti is always a hit! A lady I worked with brought this in one day, and everyone loved it. Now it is the favorite of all my dinner guests. I have made this also without the meat, and it is well received.

Baked ziti is a classic Italian-American comfort dish made with ziti pasta, layered with a rich tomato sauce, creamy ricotta or cottage cheese, mozzarella, and Parmesan. Often, it's enhanced with ground meat like beef or sausage, or kept vegetarian with added vegetables. The ingredients are layered or mixed, then baked until bubbly and golden on top. It’s a hearty, flavorful casserole-style meal that's perfect for family dinners or gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dry ziti pasta
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 2 (26 ounce) jars spaghetti sauce
  • 6 ounces provolone cheese, sliced
  • 1 ½ cups sour cream
  • 6 ounces mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Cook ziti pasta until al dente and drain.
  2. Mix cooked pasta with spaghetti sauce.
  3. In a greased baking dish, layer half the pasta, ricotta or cottage cheese, and mozzarella.
  4. Add the remaining pasta and top with Parmesan and more mozzarella.
  5. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20–25 minutes.
  6. Let cool slightly, then serve.
